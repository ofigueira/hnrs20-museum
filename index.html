Welcome

Imagine a typical day in your life. Now, imagine each and every commodity that plays a role in that day. Do you wake yourself up with a cup of coffee? Does music play on the way to class or work? Do you have a wall plastered with posters and a shelf full of your favorite books? Finally, imagine a day where all of the worldly goods you take for granted were ripped away. In a world deprived of modern technology, what would you long for? Emily St. John Mandel’s post-apocalyptic novel, Station Eleven, explores this question.

The world of Station Eleven can be characterized by the absence of spectacle. As Mandel explains, “No more diving into pools of chlorinated water lit green from below. No more ball games played out under floodlights. No more porch lights with moths fluttering on summer nights. No more trains running under the surface of cities on the dazzling power of the electric third rail. No more cities” (31). The need to preserve memories and objects from the old world intensifies with each passing year, which is why the character, Clark Thompson, creates a “Museum of Civilization” at the Severn City Airport. The museum serves as a reminder of the pre-apocalyptic world, as well as a source of comfort and curiosity for the post-apocalyptic people. Most importantly, it is a snapshot of the world that once existed.

After reading Station Eleven, our class has created our own Museum of Civilization. The Human Legacy: What They Left Behind is a collection of artifacts that resonate with each contributor individually but are also representative of society as a whole. Each artifact paints its own picture of human existence. As Station Eleven points out, “survival is insufficient,” and the artifacts in our museum are exemplary of what makes life worth living. As you navigate through our museum, we hope you ask yourself – if the world as we know it was to end, what would I miss most?

The Passion room aims to show some of the things that people used to fill the hours where they weren’t busy with more “practical things.” Passions bring us joy and relax us when we’re stressed, which is why you will find objects like gaming consoles and yoga mats in this room.
